League Rules & Regulations

Cass County Little League operates under the official rules established by Little League Baseball, Incorporated. Below are specific local adaptations and emphasized rules for the current season.

1. Pitching Limits & Rest Days

In an attempt to protect kids' arms as much as possible, new rules for pitching are being introduced this year. They are more restrictive, get used to them, know them, and follow them. Instead of pitch count, limits are governed by innings pitched.

14U Pony Division

  • No more than 5 innings in a single game.
  • No more than 7 innings in 2 days.
  • Pitching two consecutive days requires a mandatory day of rest.
  • Pitching in both games of a double-header requires a mandatory day of rest.

10U & 12U Divisions

  • No more than 4 innings in a single game.
  • No more than 6 innings in 2 days.
  • Pitching two consecutive days requires a mandatory day of rest.
  • Pitching in both games of a double-header requires a mandatory day of rest.

2. Equipment & USA Bat Standards

The league has adapted strict bat regulations to ensure player safety across all divisions. Adherence to these standards is strictly monitored.

  • 10U and 12U: USA Bats ONLY.
  • 14U: Permitted to swing either a BBCOR or a USA Bat.
Enforcement Warning: Going forward, if a player is found to be using a bat that is not approved, they will immediately be called out. Do not wait for the at-bat to finish. If identified on a pitch, anything that occurred on that specific pitch will be nullified.

3. Code of Conduct

All players, coaches, and spectators are expected to exhibit good sportsmanship at all times. Harassment of umpires, opposing players, or other spectators will result in immediate ejection from the park premises.

Questions?

If you need clarification on any league rules, please reach out to your division commissioner.
